Obituary for Jacqueline "Jackie" Frey

Jacqueline “Jackie” O. Frey, 36, of Newcastle, passed away on the morning of February 4th of a longtime illness.

Jackie was born at Miles Memorial Hospital in Damariscotta on October 5, 1979 to Sylvia (Bryant) Frey and John H. Frey III. She graduated from Lincoln Academy in the class of 1997.

While in school Jackie excelled in sports; her favorites being softball and basketball.

Jackie was an exceptionally loyal friend to everyone and always took care of them, even before herself. She was the one you’d call when you needed advice to help fix your computer, or the one you’d call when you were going through a difficult time. It never mattered what it was, she was always helping others.

She loved music and going to concerts, most recently attending a Breaking Benjamin concert with a good friend in November. She had a dry, hilarious sense of humor, that always made people laugh and made them love being around her.

Jackie had a deep love for children. She was only truly happy when she was with her two girls, Jayda and Taytum. She would spoil all of her friends’ children, and always made sure they all had what they wanted or needed.

She was an amazing friend who can never be replaced, and is deeply loved and missed by those who knew her.

Jackie was predeceased by her maternal grandparents, Ida and Guilford Bryant; paternal grandparents, Orelie “Lolly” and Frank Frey; aunts; uncles; and friends.

She is survived by her parents, John and Sylvia Frey of Newcastle; sisters, Bobbie-Jo McGraw and husband Jonathan of Newcastle, Susan Glasier and husband Scott of Fairfield, Ohio, and Katherine Lizotte and companion Henry Thompson of Newcastle; and several nieces and nephews, cousins, and friends.

A private memorial service will be held in the spring. In lieu of flowers, donations may be made to Operation HOPE.

Arrangements are under the direction and care of the Strong-Hancock Funeral Home, 612 Main Street, Damariscotta. Condolences, and messages for the family, may be expressed by visiting: www.StrongHancock.com.
